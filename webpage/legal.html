Privacy Policy

Visiting and interacting with this webpage might provide us with some of your personal data. This text describes our processing of this personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (in German DSGVO). It applies to the webpages at texstudio.org, www.texstudio.org and the deprecated mirror at texstudio.sourceforge.net. It does not apply to the project sites at github.com/texstudio-org, sourceforge.net/projects/texstudio/, mailing lists or links (e.g. to downloads) as those are external 3rd party pages on which we upload content, but we are only users of their platform.

Server log files

For technical reasons the webservers stores meta data about the accessed webpages and about your internet browser. It is stored in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR, since the data is required to provide a working, stable and secure website. The data might also be used for statistical purposes or to improve the website.

Direct mail

All mails sent to us and sent by us are processed as part of our personal correspondence and stored. They might be stored in a mail program and/or on a mail server. In accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. a GDPR sending a mail to us is freely given consent that we should process this mail. If you want to enter a contract, the mail is also processed in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. b GDPR. Storing mails long-term is necessary in case you might send additional mails referring to older mails; as well as it is in our legitimate interest to know my personal contacts in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR.

Sharing with 3rd parties

No data is shared with 3rd parties, unless this is necessary due to the following technical or legal reasons:

  • This webpage is hosted on a webserver and emails are stored on a mailserver. These servers are provided by external service providers. All communications and emails are thus processed by these service providers. These service providers might be outside the EU.
  • Emails sent by us are sent to the email provider of the recipient.
  • Mails sent as physical letters carry the address of the recipient on the outside of the envelope.
  • Legal compliance with governmental requests, court orders or similar authorities.

Storage duration and deletion

All data might be stored indefinitely. As the primary purpose of my mail address is private correspondence, it is a legitimate assumption that a correspondence might need to be continued in future.
Server logs are frequently deleted or anonymized by the external provider responsible for maintaining the servers.

Your rights

In accordance with Art. 15 GDPR and Art. 20 GDPR you can ask me about the purpose, collection and processing of your data; you have the right of rectification (e.g. Art. 16 GDPR), erasure (e.g. Art. 17 GDPR), restriction (e.g. Art. 18 GDPR), complaining to a supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR), objection and to withdraw consent (e.g. Art. 7 Abs. 3 GDPR or Art. 21 GDPR).
